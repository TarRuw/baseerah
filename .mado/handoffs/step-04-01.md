## Decisions Made

- **"Today" is the projection's own start day** (`result.points().get(0).date()`), not a second injected
  clock. In a `@SpringBootTest` the `HeuristicForecast` bean uses `Clock.systemUTC()`, so anchoring
  deficit-day math to the projection keeps it consistent with the engine that produced it and needs no
  clock wiring in `RescueService`.
- **Bounded shortfall.** The shortfall is the projected trough over `[start, deficitDate + 30d]`, not the
  raw `minProjectedBalance` — otherwise a longer assessment horizon would inflate it without bound. It
  represents "the amount to bridge ~one month of solvency."
- **Recovery curve (the engineer decision §5.4 leaves open).** `scoreAfter = scoreBefore +
  round(headroom * 0.5)`, where `headroom = 100 - scoreBefore`; MURABAHA then loses its financing overhead
  (`0.75%/month × term`), so a cost-free LIQUIDATE always recovers strictly more. Guarantees `scoreAfter >
  scoreBefore` and `liquidate > murabaha` for **any** persona. This deliberately avoids re-scoring through
  `StressScoreCalculator`: the freelancer's 90-day window has **zero dated income**, which pins the base
  score's velocity/consistency sub-scores at 0, so a re-scoring recovery model would not reliably move the
  number. The prototype's 62→78/62→74 are not hardcoded — recovery is computed from headroom + option cost.
- **`MURABAHA_MONTHLY_PROFIT_RATE` / `RECOVERY_HEADROOM_FRACTION`** are documented tunables at the top of
  `RescueService`, the same way `HeuristicForecast`/`StressScoreCalculator` expose their curve constants.

## ⚠️ Realignment of the freelancer acceptance (user-approved)

The step's prose + acceptance imply a **15-day-lead deficit alert on `client_003_freelancer`**. On the
frozen `data-mocks/` this is **not satisfiable by a faithful engine** — exactly as the Step 3.1 handoff
flagged for Phase 4. The freelancer has a single ~239k account, irregular (undated) income → no recurring
inflow, and a net drain of only ~93 SAR/day, so the base forecast first crosses zero **~2,578 days (~7
years) out**. There is no invest/fund split in the data to back a near-term deficit or the LIQUIDATE asset.

Presented the options to the user; chosen: **"Faithful engine, realign test"** (same call as Step 3.1).

**What that means in the code/test:**
- `assess(freelancer)` returns a **real deficit** (`hasDeficit=true`, positive shortfall, positive
  `deficitInDays`) but `alertRaised=false` — the 15-day window is honestly not met.
- The test asserts the **mechanics** — positive shortfall, a computed lead time, exactly two well-formed
  options, `confirm` recovers the score and logs exactly one row, and `liquidate > murabaha` — rather than
  asserting a 15-day alert. `assess(family)` asserts the healthy no-deficit path.
- Rejected alternatives (documented for the record): fabricating burn/buffer constants (violates the
  "compute from seeded transactions" Global Rule) and editing the read-only `data-mocks/` (forbidden).

**Demo implication for later phases:** if the demo needs the freelancer's alert to actually *fire* at 15
days, that requires an explicit product decision — either amend the freelancer mock (relax the read-only
rule) or add a what-if stress input (income-pause / capped-liquidity) to the assessment. Flag for Step 4.3
(Rescue UI) and Phase 7 (demo script).
