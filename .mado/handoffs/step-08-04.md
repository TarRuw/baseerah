  `RiskBadge` and the new `_DecisionChip`) so the chip sits exactly where the badge did. Added two compact

- **UI-06 ŌĆö portfolio health-status bands (`BankService.java`, `PortfolioDto.java`, `portfolio_models.dart`).**
  Root cause: the monitoring row shows `health = staminaScore` in one column but derived its status badge from
  `statusFor(app.getVerdict())` ŌĆö the **┬¦5.5 verdict**, which folds in DTI. So a facility at stamina 98 with a

  never disagree. Made `statusFor` package-private for a pure, non-DB unit test.

## Key decisions

- **UI-06 status bands ŌĆö ┬¦5.5-aligned, and flagged here for product sign-off.** DESIGN is **silent** on the
  portfolio health-status bands: ┬¦5.5 defines the underwriting *verdict* (OK/WARN/BAD over stamina+DTI), and
  the "┬¦7.6" the code's Javadoc cited **does not exist** (┬¦7 is "Flutter UI", no subsections). Per the step's

  These were confirmed with the user this session (chosen over the spec's illustrative 90/70 example precisely
  because they map onto documented policy). **ŌåÆ Product: please confirm these bands.** Cutoffs live as the
  named constants `HEALTHY_FLOOR` / `AT_RISK_CEILING` in `BankService` and are surfaced via `{@value}` in the
  Javadoc + the `PortfolioDto.Status` enum doc, so the documented bands cannot drift from the code.
- **UI-05: decision chip *replaces* the risk badge (rather than showing both).** Once a banker has acted, the
  human outcome is the salient fact and the 40px row stays uncluttered; the model verdict is still fully

  the chip; the queue is **not** re-grouped into a separate "Decided" section (the spec's "and/or" made either
  sufficient, and the chip is the lower-risk change).
